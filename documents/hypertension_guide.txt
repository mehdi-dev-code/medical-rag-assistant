HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE) GUIDE

What is Hypertension?
High blood pressure occurs when the force of blood against artery walls is consistently too high (140/90 mmHg or higher).

Blood Pressure Categories:
- Normal: Less than 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129 and less than 80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139 and 80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: 140 or higher and/or 90 or higher
- Hypertensive Crisis: Higher than 180/120 mmHg

Symptoms:
Many people with high blood pressure have NO symptoms. This is why it's called the "silent killer."
When symptoms occur:
- Severe headaches
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath
- Nosebleeds
- Vision changes
- Fatigue or confusion

Risk Factors:
- Age (risk increases with age)
- Family history
- Obesity or overweight
- Physical inactivity
- High sodium diet
- Excessive alcohol consumption
- Chronic stress
- Sleep apnea
- Smoking
- Certain medical conditions (diabetes, kidney disease)

Lifestyle Management:
1. Dietary Changes:
   - Reduce sodium intake (less than 2,300mg daily)
   - Follow DASH diet (rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains)
   - Limit alcohol
   - Reduce caffeine

2. Physical Activity:
   - 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity weekly
   - Resistance training 2-3 times per week
   - Reduce sedentary time

3. Weight Management:
   - Maintain healthy weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)
   - Each kilogram lost can lower BP by approximately 1 mmHg

4. Stress Reduction:
   - Practice relaxation techniques (meditation, yoga)
   - Get adequate sleep (7-9 hours)
   - Manage work and personal stress

5. Smoking Cessation:
   - Quit smoking immediately
   - Avoid secondhand smoke

Medications:
Common blood pressure medications include:
- ACE Inhibitors
- Beta-blockers
- Calcium Channel Blockers
- Diuretics
- Angiotensin Receptor Blockers

Monitoring:
- Check blood pressure regularly at home or doctor's office
- Keep a log of readings
- Share results with healthcare provider
- Monitor for target BP goals

Complications if Untreated:
- Heart disease
- Stroke
- Kidney disease
- Vision loss
- Erectile dysfunction

When to Seek Emergency Care:
- BP reading of 180/120 mmHg or higher with symptoms
- Severe headache, chest pain, shortness of breath
- Vision problems
- Weakness or numbness

Important Note: Always work with your healthcare provider to manage hypertension. Never stop medications without medical advice.
