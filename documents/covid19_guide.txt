COVID-19 INFORMATION AND GUIDANCE

What is COVID-19?
COVID-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus (SARS-CoV-2). It emerged in late 2019 and has become a global pandemic.

How COVID-19 Spreads:
- Person-to-person transmission through respiratory droplets
- Close contact (within 6 feet)
- Touching contaminated surfaces then face
- Airborne transmission in poorly ventilated spaces
- Can spread by asymptomatic individuals

Symptoms:
Symptoms appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus.

Common Symptoms:
- Fever or chills
- Cough (usually dry)
- Fatigue or tiredness
- Loss of taste or smell
- Difficulty breathing (in severe cases)

Other Symptoms:
- Sore throat
- Headache
- Muscle or body aches
- Congestion or runny nose
- Nausea or vomiting
- Diarrhea

Symptom Severity:
- Mild: Most people have mild symptoms
- Moderate: May include pneumonia
- Severe: Difficulty breathing, hospitalization needed
- Critical: Organ failure, ICU admission

Risk Groups for Severe Illness:
- Adults 65 and older
- People with chronic medical conditions
- Immunocompromised individuals
- Pregnant women
- Healthcare workers with repeated exposure

Prevention Measures:
1. Vaccination: Most effective prevention method
2. Masking: In crowded indoor settings
3. Hand Hygiene: Wash hands frequently with soap and water
4. Social Distancing: Maintain distance in crowds
5. Ventilation: Ensure good air circulation indoors
6. Testing: Test if symptoms develop or after exposure
7. Isolation: Stay home if sick

Treatment:
- Most people recover at home with supportive care
- Rest, fluids, and fever management
- Medications for symptom relief
- Antiviral medications in high-risk patients (if caught early)
- Hospitalization for severe cases

When to Seek Medical Care:
- Trouble breathing
- Persistent chest pain or pressure
- Confusion
- Inability to wake or stay awake
- Pale, gray, or blue-colored skin, lips, or nail beds

Vaccination Information:
- Multiple vaccines available (Pfizer, Moderna, J&J, etc.)
- Highly effective at preventing severe illness
- Recommended for all 6 months and older
- Booster shots available for additional protection
- Safe and widely administered

Long COVID:
Some people experience lingering symptoms weeks or months after infection:
- Fatigue
- Shortness of breath
- Brain fog
- Joint or muscle pain
- Consult healthcare provider if symptoms persist

Testing:
- Rapid antigen tests available at home
- PCR tests (more accurate) at healthcare facilities
- Positive tests indicate active infection

Quarantine and Isolation Guidelines:
- Isolate at home if positive or symptomatic
- Duration depends on severity and vaccination status
- Consult CDC or local health department for current guidelines

Important Note: This information is educational. Follow your local health department and healthcare provider's guidance for current recommendations.
