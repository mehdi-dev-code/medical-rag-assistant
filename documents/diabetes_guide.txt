DIABETES MANAGEMENT GUIDE

What is Diabetes?
Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar levels.

Types of Diabetes:
1. Type 1 Diabetes - Autoimmune condition where pancreas doesn't produce insulin
2. Type 2 Diabetes - Body becomes resistant to insulin
3. Gestational Diabetes - Occurs during pregnancy

Symptoms of Diabetes:
- Increased thirst and frequent urination
- Unexplained weight loss
- Fatigue and weakness
- Blurred vision
- Numbness or tingling in hands/feet
- Slow healing of cuts and wounds
- Irritability or mood changes

Risk Factors:
- Family history of diabetes
- Obesity or being overweight
- Age (especially over 45)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Unhealthy diet high in sugar
- High blood pressure

Management and Treatment:
1. Blood Sugar Monitoring: Check levels regularly as directed by doctor
2. Healthy Diet: Focus on whole grains, vegetables, lean proteins
3. Regular Exercise: 150 minutes per week of moderate activity
4. Weight Management: Maintain healthy weight through diet and exercise
5. Medication: Take prescribed medications as directed
6. Regular Check-ups: Monitor kidney function, eye health, foot care

Prevention:
- Maintain healthy weight
- Eat balanced, nutritious diet
- Exercise regularly
- Reduce stress
- Get adequate sleep
- Limit alcohol consumption
- Avoid smoking

When to See a Doctor:
- Persistent thirst or frequent urination
- Unexplained weight loss
- Fatigue or weakness
- Vision changes
- Numbness in extremities

Important Note: This is educational information. Always consult with healthcare professionals for proper diagnosis and treatment.
